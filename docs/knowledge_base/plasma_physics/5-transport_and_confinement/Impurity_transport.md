# Impurity Transport

## Abstract

!!! abstract "Forces Acting on Imprities"
    The main forces acting on impurities inside a plasma are driven by: friction with bulk plasma flow and temperature gradients. 
    Other forces include: impurity concentration, electric field effects and cross-field transport.

    The following discussion is valid for trace impurities.

## Forces Acting on Impurities in the Edge Plasma

In fusion plasmas, the aim is usually to avoid ingress of radiating impurities into the core plasma. On the contrary, the presence of impurities in the SOL and divertor is foreseen to reduce power flux to the divertor plates. 

### Frictional Drag

This force is proportional to the relative velocity between the species and inversely proportional to the stopping time, which scales with the plasma density.

The plasma ions typically flow from the core towards the divertor targets. When impurities come into contact with the stram of the bulk plasma, a frictional force arising from momentum exchange drags neutral particles towards the targets.

However, if recycling arises, there can be a backflow of the main ion species, that can induce a frictional drag from the target towards the core. In principle, this can be avoided by augmenting the bulk plasma ion flow in the SOL sufficiently (for example using gas puffing).

### Temperature Gradient

Since the cross-section for momentum transfer decreases strongly with the relative speed of colliding particles, the (Coulomb) ions and electron collision frequencies have an inverse dependence on temperature. This means that colder plasma ions (near the target) exchange more momentum with the impurities than the hotter ions (near the core).

In the local fluid frame, particles coming from opposite sides would cancel in a homogeneous plasma; with a temperature gradient, the hotter side particles collide less effectively than the colder side particles, this results in a net force driving impurities away from the target and towards the core plasma.
